How It Works

The Basics

The Open-Access Checker takes in your funder and institution, along with a BibTeX file containing a bibliography of academic journals, in order to check the Open-Access compliance of these journals with your funder and institution.

API

The Open-Access Checker makes calls to an existing “Journal Checker Tool” API, but in batch form, allowing for researchers to check more journals more quickly, but using the same background functionality.

Results

When the Open-Access Checker has finished checking the open-access compliance of your submitted journals, funder, and institution, it displays the percentage of these journals which are fully open-access compliant, along with the percentage that may be open access compliant in the future.

A journal's potential future open-access compliance is based on whether it is a transformative journal, it’s publisher has entered into a transformative agreement with your institution, or it is in self-archiving for open-access publishing at a later date.

Journals are clearly highlighted in either:

  • GREEN (fully open-access),
  • RED (not open-access),
  • or YELLOW (may be open-access in the future).
