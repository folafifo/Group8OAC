FAQs

What is the Open Access Checker for?

The Open Access Checker is provided to authors to support them in finding Open-Access compliant routes through which to publish their articles. The tool allows an author to enter their funder and institution, along with a bibliography of journals, and checks if these combinations of funder, institution and journal offer any route to Open-Access compliance.

What file formats can I upload my bibliography in?

The Open-Access checker accepts only BibTeX file uploads, however, it also offers the option to upload a BibTeX formatted text submission.

I can't find my publication via its ISBN

The Open Access Checker only supports checks on serials that are identified by ISSN. If a book or conference series has an ISSN you can try searching with that, however, if it does not, you will not be able to determine your Open-Access compliance via this tool.

What is a Transformative Journal?

A Transformative Journal is a subscription/hybrid journal that is committed to transitioning to a fully OA journal. In addition, it must:

  • gradually increase the share of OA content and
  • offset subscription income from payments for publishing services (to avoid double payments).

What is a Transformative Agreement?

The global Open Access 2020 Initiative (OA2020) aims to accelerate the transition to Open Access by adopting strategies to systematically withdraw financial support of paywalled publishing venues and reinvest those funds to support Open Access publishing.

Publishers are encouraged to enter into transformative arrangements globally in all countries and share data from such arrangements. These agreements are essentially a promise that the publishers will push toward transformative journals, and eventually full open-access publishing.
